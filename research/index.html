Research Program

Focused on technologies that are safer, simpler, and more accessible.

The research efforts of The ZSMC Co. are centered on identifying practical engineering pathways that address real-world technical limitations. Projects are selected based on scientific merit, scalability potential, material accessibility, and opportunities for experimental validation.

Current work is concentrated on zinc-based electrochemical storage systems, with future investigations planned across adjacent engineering and deep-technology domains.

Current Research Initiatives

Flagship Project

The ZSMC Battery Architecture

Project Lead: Srijal Dutta

The ZSMC Battery Architecture is a research initiative focused on the development of zinc-based electrochemical storage systems. The project investigates architecture design, performance characteristics, validation pathways, failure modes, and long-term deployment opportunities.

Research Principles

How projects are evaluated and developed.

Evidence First

Technical claims should be supported by mechanisms, calculations, experimental data, or clearly stated assumptions.

Transparent Development

Research progress, limitations, and future work are documented openly whenever possible.

Engineering Practicality

Material abundance, manufacturing feasibility, and deployment considerations are treated as core design constraints.

Continuous Validation

Concepts are refined through modeling, testing, review, and iterative improvement.

The ZSMC Co. is an independent research initiative focused on scientific investigation, technical development, and experimental validation across emerging technologies and engineering disciplines.
